Gate Movement Styles

Automated gates move in different ways depending on your driveway layout, available space, and site conditions. Thomas Fence installs all major gate movement types:

Swing Gate

The classic automated gate — one or two panels swing inward or outward. Best for properties with adequate driveway depth behind the gate line and relatively level terrain.

Cantilever Slide Gate

Slides horizontally without ground contact — preferred where track drainage is an issue or where the driveway surface can't support a V-track. Handles heavy gate weights reliably.

V-Track Slide Gate

Rolls on a V-groove track embedded in the ground. Cost-effective for large openings where the site conditions support a ground track installation.

Vertical Pivot Gate

Pivots vertically — opens by tilting upward rather than swinging or sliding. Useful for sites with restricted lateral space or narrow driveways where swing clearance isn't available.

Vertical Lift Gate

Rises straight up — ideal for high-security applications and locations where space on either side of the opening is at a premium. Commonly used in industrial and perimeter security applications.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can any gate be automated?

Most gate styles can be automated — swing, slide, cantilever, and others. The key factors are gate weight, opening width, and available power. We assess your site during a consultation to confirm feasibility and recommend the appropriate operator for your specific gate.

What power source do automated gates need?

Most operators run on standard 120V AC power. For locations where running power to the gate is impractical, solar-powered operators are available — particularly for residential swing gates in sunny climates. We evaluate power options during the site assessment.

What safety devices are required?

UL 325 requires entrapment protection on all automated gates — typically safety edges, photo eyes, or loop detectors depending on gate type. Thomas Fence installs all required safety devices as part of every gate automation project, not as add-ons.

Do I need a permit for an automated gate?

Permit requirements vary by municipality. In general, gates with electrical components are more likely to require a permit than manual gates. Thomas Fence will advise you on local requirements for your specific city — the customer is responsible for obtaining permits, and we're happy to assist with documentation.

Can you integrate gate automation with a smart home or security system?

Many modern operators support smart home integration via Z-Wave, Wi-Fi, or app-based control. We can discuss compatible systems during your consultation. For advanced security integration, we coordinate with your security provider as needed.

What does CGAT certification mean?

CGAT stands for Certified Gate Automation Technician — a designation from the American Fence Association (AFA). It requires demonstrated knowledge of gate operator mechanics, UL 325 safety standards, access control systems, and safe installation practices. It's the recognized industry credential for gate automation work.
