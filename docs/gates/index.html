Why CGAT Certification Matters

The CGAT (Certified Gate Automation Technician) designation is issued by the American Fence Association and requires demonstrated knowledge of gate operator mechanics, UL 325 safety standards, access control systems, and installation best practices.

It's the recognized industry credential for gate automation — and it matters because automated gates are heavy, fast-moving machines. An improperly installed gate operator without correct safety devices can cause serious injury. CGAT-certified installers know the required entrapment protection, force limits, and safety device placement for every gate type.

Gate FAQs

What types of gates does Thomas Fence install?

Thomas Fence installs swing gates, cantilever slide gates, V-track slide gates, vertical pivot gates, and vertical lift gates — in ornamental steel, aluminum, vinyl, chain link, and wood. Every movement style is available for both residential and commercial properties.

Can any gate be automated?

Most swing and slide gates can be automated if properly sized and if post framing is adequate. Thomas Fence evaluates automation-readiness during the estimate visit. When we build a new gate, we design it with automation in mind from the start so there are no surprises later.

What does gate automation include?

A complete automated gate system includes the operator (motor), UL 325-required safety devices (photo eyes, safety edges, or loop detectors depending on gate type), access control (keypad, remote, or phone entry), and all wiring. Thomas Fence handles the complete installation — no subcontracting.

What is CGAT certification and why does it matter?

The Certified Gate Automation Technician (CGAT) designation is issued by the American Fence Association. It requires demonstrated knowledge of gate operator mechanics, UL 325 safety standards, and access control systems. Automated gates are heavy, fast-moving machines — an improperly installed operator without correct safety devices can cause serious injury. Thomas Fence holds this certification. Most NWA fence contractors do not.

How long does a gate installation take?

A standard residential gate installation typically takes 1–3 business days. Automated gate systems may take slightly longer depending on access control requirements and concrete curing time for posts. Thomas Fence will give you a timeline estimate before work begins.

Is a deposit required for gate installation?

No deposit is required for standard gate installations. Special order materials or custom metal fabrication may require a deposit before work begins. Thomas Fence will communicate any deposit requirement upfront in your contract.

Do I need a permit for a gate in Northwest Arkansas?

Permit requirements vary by city. Most cities in NWA require a permit for any new fence or gate installation. Thomas Fence is happy to advise on the permit process — pulling the permit is the property owner's responsibility. See our Fence Permits page for links to local permit offices.

What access control options are available?

Thomas Fence installs PIN keypads, remote transmitters, intercom systems, and app-controlled entry (LiftMaster MyQ and similar). We help you choose the right system for your property type, security level, and number of users.
