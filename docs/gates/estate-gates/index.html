Estate Gate Materials

Each material brings different strengths. Thomas Fence installs all four in Northwest Arkansas, and our team can help you choose based on your property's style, your budget, and how much maintenance you want to take on.

Ornamental Steel

Available in: Flat Top, Spear Top, Double Drive, Walk Gate

The most popular choice for NWA estate gate clients. Powder-coated steel holds up through Arkansas summers and winters with minimal maintenance. Ornamental steel can be fabricated in virtually any panel design and is the strongest option for high-traffic driveways.

Vinyl

Available in: Privacy Panel, Picket, Estate Style

Vinyl estate gates never need painting and won't rot or rust. They're a top pick for residential properties with existing vinyl fencing. Color-matched panels create a seamless, polished look from the street.

Chain Link

Available in: Single Drive, Double Drive, Cantilever Slide

Chain link estate gates are the most cost-effective option and are well-suited for rural properties, farm access points, and commercial driveways that prioritize function over aesthetics. Galvanized and vinyl-coated options are available.

Wood

Available in: Board-on-Board, Horizontal Privacy, Cedar Picket

Cedar and pressure-treated wood gates bring a natural, warm look to residential driveways and garden entries. Wood gates require more upkeep than vinyl or steel, but many homeowners prefer their character. Staining should be done by a separate contractor — we recommend waiting 1–3 months after installation for pressure-treated wood; cedar can be stained immediately.

Swinging vs. Sliding — What's Right for Your Property?

The two most common estate gate configurations each have real-world advantages. Your driveway grade, available clearance, and automation plans all factor into which direction makes more sense for your property.

Swing Gates

Swing gates open inward or outward on hinges and are the most traditional estate gate style. They work best on level driveways with enough clearance for the gate arc. Single and double drive configurations are both available. Swing gates are compatible with most gate operators and are the most common residential choice in NWA.

Slide Gates

Slide gates roll parallel to the fence line instead of swinging out — ideal for sloped driveways or tight approaches where a swing gate would struggle. Cantilever slide gates don't require a track on the ground, which keeps them reliable in all weather. Heavy-duty commercial slide gates are built to handle high-cycle traffic.

Estate Gate FAQs

What is an estate gate?

An estate gate is a large, often decorative gate system installed at the primary entry point of a property — typically a driveway entrance. Estate gates are distinguished from standard fence gates by their width (usually 10–20+ feet for double drives), heavier-duty construction, and the option for automated operation. They serve both a security function and a strong aesthetic statement.

Can an estate gate be automated?

Yes — most estate gate systems can be automated at the time of installation or retrofitted with a gate operator later. Thomas Fence is CGAT-certified (Certified Gate Automation Technician) and installs both residential and commercial-grade operators. Automation options include keypad entry, remote controls, vehicle loop detectors, and video intercom systems. See our Gate Automation page for full details.

What material is best for an estate gate in Northwest Arkansas?

Ornamental steel is the most popular choice for NWA estate gate clients due to its strength, low maintenance, and wide range of design options. Vinyl is a close second for residential properties that want a clean look without painting. Wood brings a natural character that many homeowners prefer, though it requires more upkeep. Chain link is the most economical option and is common on rural and farm properties. The right material depends on your property style, budget, and intended use — our team will give you a straight recommendation during your free estimate.

How wide does an estate gate need to be?

Standard double-drive estate gates range from 12 to 20 feet wide to accommodate most vehicles. For properties with large trucks, RVs, or farm equipment, 16–24 feet is common. Single-drive configurations typically run 10–14 feet. Walk gates are often added alongside a drive gate for pedestrian entry. Thomas Fence measures your driveway and accounts for post placement and automation hardware clearances during the estimate.

How long does estate gate installation take?

Most residential estate gate installations are completed in one to two days. Larger systems with automated operators, concrete post work, or access control components may take two to three days. Thomas Fence calls 811 before any digging (required by Arkansas state law) and coordinates that 3-day utility locate window before scheduling your install date.

Can you customize the design of an estate gate?

Yes. Ornamental steel gates in particular can be fabricated in custom panel designs, heights, and finishes. Vinyl gates are available in multiple colors and styles. Wood gates can be built in any panel configuration. During your estimate, your sales representative will walk through available design options and what can be ordered or fabricated for your property.
