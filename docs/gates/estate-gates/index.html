Material Options

Estate gates are available in four materials. Each can be built as a swinging gate or a sliding gate depending on your driveway layout and available space.

Metal (Ornamental Steel)

The most popular choice for estate gates. Ornamental steel gates offer an elegant, architectural look with maximum durability. Custom picket patterns and scrollwork available. Powder-coated finish resists rust and holds its color for years.

Available as: Swinging Gate · Sliding Gate

Vinyl

Clean lines, low maintenance, and a bright white or almond finish that won't fade, rot, or need painting. Vinyl estate gates work well for residential properties that want a classic picket or board style at the entrance.

Available as: Swinging Gate · Sliding Gate

Chain Link

A practical choice for agricultural, commercial, or utility entrances where security and durability matter more than aesthetics. Available in galvanized or PVC-coated finishes. Holds up in high-traffic applications.

Available as: Swinging Gate · Sliding Gate

Wood

Cedar and pressure-treated pine gates that match a wood fence perimeter. Wood gates have natural warmth and character — cedar can be stained immediately; pressure-treated pine should cure 1–3 months before staining.

Available as: Swinging Gate · Sliding Gate

Swinging vs. Sliding: Which Is Right for You?

Swinging Gate

One or two panels that swing inward or outward. Classic look, simpler hardware, and easier to automate. Requires adequate driveway depth behind the gate line and relatively level terrain. Best for residential driveways with flat approaches.

Sliding Gate

The gate panel slides horizontally along the fence line rather than swinging out. Ideal when driveway space is limited, the approach is on a slope, or the gate opening is very wide. Cantilever and V-track options available.

Not Sure?

Your site conditions — driveway grade, available clearance, and property layout — determine which style works. We assess your property during the free consultation and recommend the right configuration before any commitment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is an estate gate?

An estate gate is a large, custom entrance gate — typically for a driveway or property entrance — designed to make a statement as well as provide security. Estate gates are generally wider and taller than standard gates, built from premium materials, and are often automated. They're common at residential estates, farms, and commercial properties that want a polished entrance.

Can an estate gate be automated?

Yes — virtually all estate gate styles and materials can be automated. Thomas Fence holds the CGAT certification and installs automated gate systems as part of the same project. We handle the gate, the operator, safety devices, and access control so you work with one contractor from start to finish.

What material is best for an estate gate?

Ornamental steel is the most popular choice — it offers the best combination of strength, elegance, and longevity with minimal maintenance. Vinyl is a great low-maintenance option with a clean residential look. Wood adds natural warmth and pairs well with wood fence perimeters. Chain link is practical for agricultural and commercial applications. We'll help you weigh the trade-offs during your consultation.

How wide should my driveway gate be?

For a single-vehicle driveway, a 12-foot gate is generally sufficient. For two-lane driveways or properties with larger vehicles — trucks, RVs, farm equipment — 16 to 20 feet is common. We measure your driveway during the site visit and recommend an appropriate width based on your vehicles and usage.

How long does estate gate installation take?

A standard estate gate installation typically takes 1–2 days for the gate itself. If automation is added, allow an additional day for the operator, wiring, and access control components. Lead time from signed contract to installation start is typically 1–3 weeks depending on scheduling.

Can I customize the design?

Yes. For ornamental steel gates, we can incorporate custom picket styles, scrollwork, and finishes. For wood gates, we work with your fence style and stain preferences. Vinyl gates come in standard panel styles. During the estimate process, we work through the design details with you so the gate matches your property and your expectations.
