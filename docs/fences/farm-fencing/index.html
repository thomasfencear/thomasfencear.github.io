Springdale Arkansas Farm Fencing

Farm fencing is popular for wide expanses of agricultural land, animal farms, and residential communities too. Strong rail farm fences are the perfect option for protecting crops or keeping your animals safe on your property.

Thomas Fence has been serving all of Northwest Arkansas for over 32 years. There are many farm fencing options you can select to create the perfect solution for your situation. Choose post-and-rail fences with 2, 3, or 4 rails based on your needs and preferences, and add wire mesh for added security.

Most Popular Farm Fence Options

2-Rail Farm Fences

The shortest of the farm fencing options, the 2-rail fence is just like it sounds with 2 wooden rails fixed to each post around your property. Ideal for marking boundaries and lighter containment.

3-Rail Farm Fences

For more height and an extra rail for strength and protection, 3-rail farm fences are probably the most common type of agricultural fencing. A strong, practical choice for most farm and ranch applications.

4-Rail Farm Fences

Choose your farm fencing based on the aesthetics you are looking for as well as the height that you need. A 4-rail fence provides maximum height and strength for large animals or high-security perimeters.

Buckley Fence

Buckley Fence delivers a durable, low-maintenance system providing safety, flexibility, and visibility. Key advantages:

  • Designed to flex on impact without injury to animals
  • High visibility to prevent animals from running into the fence
  • Low-maintenance — no painting, no rot, no splinters
  • Ideal for horse paddocks, cattle operations, and large-perimeter farms

Wire Mesh Add-On

Add wire mesh to any post-and-rail fence for additional security. Wire mesh is especially useful for smaller animals, preventing them from squeezing through the rails. We can add mesh to new installations or incorporate it into your existing rail fence.

Farm Fence FAQs

How long will it take to get my farm fence installed?

Generally, it only takes a few days for one of our fence experts to get to most areas of Northwest Arkansas, walk the property, and provide you with an accurate estimate. Once you approve the estimate, it takes around 1 to 3 weeks to begin the fence installation. Large-perimeter projects may take additional time — our team will give you a realistic timeline up front.

What is the best farm fence for large animals like horses and cattle?

For horses and large cattle, we typically recommend a 4-rail fence or Buckley Fence. Buckley Fence is specifically engineered to flex on impact, reducing injury risk to animals. The high visibility of Buckley Fence also helps prevent animals from running into it accidentally. We can add wire mesh to any rail fence for additional containment of smaller animals.

Do you install farm fences on large rural properties?

Yes. Thomas Fence installs farm fencing on properties of all sizes across Northwest Arkansas — from small hobby farms to large agricultural operations with hundreds of acres to fence. We are experienced with the terrain and soil conditions throughout Benton and Washington counties. Contact us for a free consultation and estimate on your large-perimeter project.

Does Thomas Fence require a deposit for farm fencing?

No deposit is required for standard installations. The exception is special-order materials. Thomas Fence prefers to wait until the job is completed before requesting payment.
