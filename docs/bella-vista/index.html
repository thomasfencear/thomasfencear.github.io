Fencing in a Planned Community With Its Own Rules

Bella Vista is unlike any other city in Northwest Arkansas. Originally developed as a planned community by Cooper Communities beginning in the 1960s, it became an incorporated city in 2006 — but the Bella Vista Property Owners Association (POA) has governed the community for decades and continues to do so. The POA maintains its own covenants and architectural guidelines that apply to many properties throughout the city, including requirements around fencing. Height limits, material restrictions, placement relative to setbacks, and visibility from the street can all factor into whether a fence is approved — and the rules can vary by neighborhood and lot type.

The city itself is defined by its natural landscape. The mayor's office describes it as a community where "the forested landscape boasts surprising bluffs, blue-green creeks, waterfalls and more." Bella Vista has more than 100 miles of trails for hiking and mountain biking, multiple lakes for fishing and recreation, and a character that draws residents who want wooded lots, outdoor access, and a quieter pace than the cities to the south. Many of those lots — with mature trees, uneven terrain, and irregular boundaries — present real challenges for fence installation that require an experienced crew familiar with the area.

Thomas Fence has been working on Bella Vista properties since 1993. We know how to work around tree roots, rocky ground, and sloped terrain common in this part of Benton County. We are happy to advise on fence types that typically meet Bella Vista POA guidelines, and we strongly recommend getting your POA approval in writing before installation begins. Our estimates are free, and no deposit is required for standard residential installations.

Bella Vista Fencing FAQs

Does the Bella Vista POA have fencing rules I need to follow?

Yes. The Bella Vista Property Owners Association (POA) maintains architectural guidelines and covenants that apply to many properties in the community. These can include restrictions on fence height, materials, placement relative to setbacks, and visibility from the street. Requirements can vary by neighborhood and lot type. We recommend contacting the Bella Vista POA directly for your specific property guidelines and getting written approval before installation begins.

Is a city permit also required in Bella Vista?

Yes. In addition to any POA approval, the City of Bella Vista may require a permit for fence installations depending on fence height and location. Our fence permits page has information on permit requirements throughout Northwest Arkansas. We recommend checking with both the POA and the city permit office before proceeding.

Can you install fencing on sloped or wooded lots?

Yes. Thomas Fence has been working on Bella Vista properties since 1993 and is experienced with the terrain conditions common in this part of Benton County — sloped ground, rocky soil, tree roots, and irregular lot boundaries. We assess all of these conditions during the estimate visit and account for them in the project plan.

How soon can installation start after I sign?

Once your contract is signed, your project goes on the schedule — typically within 1 to 3 weeks. Before any digging begins, Thomas Fence contacts 811 for a utility locate, which takes approximately 3 working days. We coordinate this on your behalf once the contract is in place.

Is a deposit required?
